# How to update your browser

### Google Chrome
1. Launch the Chrome browser on your computer.
2. Click the three dots in the top right corner to open the menu.
3. Hover over `Help` in the menu.
4. Click on `About Google Chrome`.
5. If you can't find this button, you're on the latest version.
6. After the update is downloaded, click `Relaunch` to restart Chrome and complete the update.

### Firefox
1. Launch the Firefox browser on your computer.
2. Click the three horizontal lines in the top right corner to access the menu.
3. Choose `Help` from the dropdown menu.
4. Click on `About Firefox`.
5. A pop-up window will appear with the version of Firefox.  If there is a newer version of Firefox, you can click on the button to install it.  You may need to restart Firefox for the new version to become active.
6. If an update is downloaded, click `Restart to update Firefox` to complete the process.

### Microsoft Edge
1. Launch Microsoft Edge on your computer.
2. Click the three dots in the top right corner to access the menu.
3. Select `Help and feedback` from the menu.
4. Choose `About Microsoft Edge`.
5. Edge will automatically check for updates. If an update is available, it will download automatically.
6. After the update is downloaded, click `Restart` to apply the update.

### Safari
1. Open the Apple menu by clicking the apple icon in the top left corner of your screen.
2. Choose `System Preferences`.
3. Click on `Software Update`.
4. Your Mac will check for updates, including Safari. If there's an update available, click `Update Now` or `Upgrade Now`.
5. Follow the onscreen instructions to complete the update process.

**Note:** Safari updates are tied to macOS updates. Ensure all important data is backed up before updating your system.
